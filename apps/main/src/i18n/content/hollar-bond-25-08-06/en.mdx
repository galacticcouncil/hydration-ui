HOLLARb is a fixed-term bond strategy designed to grow stablecoin liquidity for HOLLAR. Users purchase HOLLAR bonds using supported stablecoins such as aUSDC or aUSDT, receiving HOLLAR at a discount compared to its maturity value.

At maturity, each bond redeems **1:1 for HOLLAR**. The user’s yield comes from the difference between the discounted purchase price and the full HOLLAR amount received at redemption. For example, a **3-month bond at 6.9% APR gives a 1.725%** return over the term, so 1,000 stablecoins would redeem for **1,017.25 HOLLAR** before fees.

Bond terms are fixed upfront, including APR, maturity date, available capacity, fees, and expected redemption amount. Once purchased, the position remains locked until maturity unless secondary market or early-exit functionality is supported.

The stablecoins collected through bond sales help deepen HOLLAR liquidity and support treasury strategies across selected stablecoin and HOLLAR markets.

### Risk management

Hollar bonds are fixed-term positions and are not the same as instant-withdrawal vault deposits. Users should understand the maturity date, redemption asset, and applicable fees before purchasing.

Key risks include smart contract risk, treasury execution risk, liquidity risk, stablecoin risk, and the ability of the system to honor HOLLAR redemption at maturity. If bonds are sold via OTC or proxy accounts, operational and multisig controls should also be considered.

The product should clearly show gross APR, net yield after fees, maturity date, amount paid, HOLLAR receivable at maturity, and whether early exit is available. This helps users understand both the expected return and the conditions required to realize it.
